Our Council Activities

Knights of Columbus Council 10653 has a regular program of activities that will interest all Catholic men at Holy Spirit Parish. These activities and projects will provide volunteer opportuntiies that cover a broad range of areas that include: Church, Vocations, Family, Youth, Pro-Life, and community.

Your active participation as a Knight in these activities will benefit you and strengthen your Church, the Community, and Council 10653. As Knights, our goal is to emphasize the key principles of the Order; Charity, Unity, Fraternity, and Patriotism. You are invited to help in accomplishing these goals. Our Council's most notable activities are listed below.

Our Council Meetings


Council meetings are held twice a month on the second and fourth Tuesdays at the Parish of the Holy Spirit located at 7416 W. Clearwater Avenue, in Kennewick. These meetings are the regular business meetings of the Council with reports of activities, refreshments and fellowship with Brother Knights following the meetings.

We also host an open Council meeting once a quarter to provide a council or parish forum for invited speakers, video, and movies of special interest.

Our Council Social Events and Activities


  • Sponsoring trips to Mariner's game
  • Sponsoring Council Golf Tournaments
  • Hosting Council "Knights" at Tri-City America's Hockey games
  • Sponsoring Annual Trip to Leavenworth for shopping and Christmas Tree lighting Ceremonies
  • Sponsoring Annual Council Appreciation Dinner to honor the contributions of our Council Members and Spouses
  • An annual memorial Mass is offered, in November, for the repose of the souls of our departed Brother Knights. Attendance of our Brother Knights and families is encouraged.
  • Socializing after Council Meetings
  • Annual Council Christmas Party

Our Church-Related Activities/Projects


  • Hosting cookies and coffee at all masses on the Second Sunday of each month
  • Hosting a Weekly rosary on Thursday nights
  • Council Choir at 8:30 Mass on on the Second Sunday of each month
  • Hosting Annual Marion Hour of Prayer with National K of C Traveling Icon Program
  • Hosting Lenten Soup Knight
  • Spring and Fall church cleanup
  • Helping sponsor Annual Parish Summer Picnic
  • Keeping Christ in Christmas by providing Parish community with Religious Cards at Christmas

Our Vocation-Related Activities


  • Council members regularly donate pocket change to a "Pennies for Heaven" fund. In addition, special activities generate additional funds for the program. Interest from this fund (about $60,000) is divided equally between the Yakima, Spokane and Seattle Dioceses and used by the Bishops for education of new Priests.
  • Direct Annual Financial Support to Seminarians and others undergoing Religious Educations through our Refund Support for Vocations Program (RSVP).
  • Praying for Vocations at every Council meeting

Our Parish Family and Youth Activities


  • Annual Christmas Adopt-a-Family Program
  • Support to Parish Youth Basketball Programs
  • Annual $2000 Scholarship to St. Joseph's Catholic School
  • Annual Children's Halloween Party
  • Annual Children's Christmas Party
  • Council-sponsored in the Basketball Free-throw Competition open to children of Knights and the Parish. Winners of this competition go on to compete at the District and State levels.

Our Community-Related Activities


  • Council support to KC HELP Program
  • Annual Sprinkler Blowout Fundraiser
  • Council-sponsored Pantry Sunday to enhance Parish support and contributions to the Food Bank

  • Council-sponsored Waffle Cone Booth Fund-Raiser at:
    • St. Joseph's MAYFEST (May)

Council Recognition

Knight of the Year

Each year an award is made to a Knight for outstanding service to the Council. This award is traditionally presented to the Knight of the Year at the annual Officer Installation and Dinner. Our Knight of the Year for 2005-2006 is Brother Gerry McHenry.

Gerry holds the office of Recorder of our Council, and also serves as the Youth Chairman. He attends all the meetings and performs the duties that are associated with those positions. Gerry is also a 4th Degree Sir Knight. With Gerry, it is very difficult to separate Gerry’s involvement in the Knights of Columbus without including his family. Gerry and his family are found at most of the Knight’s fund raising events, such as working in our ice cream booth when it is in operation at the St. Joseph’s Mayfest, the Waterfollies, and the Benton County Fair. Gerry and his family are always involved in the Knight’s Mother’s Day and Father’s Day pancake breakfasts, which we sponsor for our Parish. Gerry and his daughter Casey are members of our Knight’s choir. Gerry and his family are frequent participants when the Knights serve refreshments after all the Masses on the 2nd Sunday of every month. Gerry and his wife Laraine organize the St. Nicholas party that the Knights sponsor for the children of our Parish at Christmas.

The Council thanks Gerry for his unconditional service to his family, Council and community. God Bless him for strongly sharing his time and talents.

Family of the Year

Each year an award is made to a Family for outstanding service to the Council. This award is traditionally presented to the Family of the Year at the annual Officer Installation and Dinner. Our Council has named the Larry and Rita Cleveland Family as 2005-2006 Family of the Year.

Larry has been a member of the Knights of Columbus for 35 years, first joining the Knights in Clarkston Washington and transferred to the Richland Council upon moving to the Tri-Cities. Living in Kennewick, he became a charter member with the Kennewick Council and then became a charter member with Holy Spirit Council in its beginning year 1991. He was instrumental in getting Holy Spirit Council going and held many positions up to being Grand Knight in fraternal year 2000-2001. He is a very active member of our Council and is also a 4th Degree Knight. Larry and Rita are found at most of the Knight’s fund raising events, such as working in our ice cream booth when it is in operation at the St. Joseph’s Mayfest, the Waterfollies, and the Benton County Fair. Larry was instrumental in securing the financial future of the Council by his dedication to the construction of the ice cream booth. The booth, built over a nine-month period in 1998, was dedicated to the memory of Jim Nichols who left the Council funds with which to build the booth. The construction used over 1,400 man hours, 1,000 miles worth of driving to pick up materials, and the assistance of 25 Knights at various times.

Larry and Rita have raised four children in the Catholic tradition; two sons and two daughters, all graduates of St. Joseph’s school. They now have nine grandchildren that keep them more than busy. After years of supporting their children in their sports and other activities, they now attend most of their local grandchildren’s events and like to travel to support the out of town grandchildren when possible.

Larry and Rita have raised a very devout Catholic family. They have sent all their children to Catholic Grade School. They attend Mass together every Sunday and many weekdays when possible. Their actions exemplify Catholic Christian morals and values. This Council is proud to name the Cleveland family as Family of the Month and Year.

Council Spirit Award

Each year an award is made to a Knight who personifies the spirit and ideals of the Knights of Columbus with sustained and committed service the Council for outstanding service to the Council. This award is traditionally presented at the annual Officer Installation and Dinner. Our Spirit Award recipient is Brother Knight Ron Mertens for his outstanding work he did during the 2005-2006 fraternal year.
